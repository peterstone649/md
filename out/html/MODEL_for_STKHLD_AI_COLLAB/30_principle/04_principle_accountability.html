4. Principle of Accountability (PRIN_MSHCOL_ACCOUNTABILITY) [PRIO: CRITICAL]

Version: V0.1.0 Status: OPEN Date: 2026-01-09

We establish accountability as a core principle requiring clear responsibility chains for all stakeholder-AI collaborative outputs.

Objectives: 1. Responsibility Clarity: Define who owns each decision and output 2. Traceability: Enable tracking of responsibility through workflows 3. Consequence Management: Support appropriate responses to outcomes 4. Governance: Enable effective oversight of stakeholder-AI collaboration

Abstract

[AI_LOCK] The Principle of Accountability establishes that in stakeholder-AI collaboration, clear chains of responsibility must exist for all outputs, decisions, and actions. Neither AI systems nor human stakeholders can operate without identifiable accountability structures. This principle ensures that when outcomes occur—whether positive or negative—there is always a clear path to understanding who or what contributed, enabling appropriate responses, learning, and governance. [END_AI_LOCK]

1. Scope and Applicability

1.1 When to Apply

  • All collaborative outputs are produced
  • AI recommendations influence decisions
  • Quality issues or errors occur
  • Governance and oversight are required

1.2 Target Audience

  • All stakeholders in collaborative workflows
  • Governance bodies
  • Quality assurance teams
  • AI system operators

4. Rules and Guidelines

4.1 Accountability Requirements

  1. Clear Ownership: Every output has an identified owner
  2. Contribution Tracking: Document AI and human contributions
  3. Decision Logging: Record who approved each significant decision
  4. Outcome Documentation: Track results and their attributions

4.2 Responsibility Matrix

Content Creation Human Stakeholder Quality Review Assistant
Decision Approval Human Stakeholder Governance Body Recommender
