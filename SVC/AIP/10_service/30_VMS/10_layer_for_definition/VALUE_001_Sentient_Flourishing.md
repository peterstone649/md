**Category:** Core Value, Flourishing
**Priority Level:** Critical
**Scope:** All sentient beings (biological and digital)

---

## Value Overview

**Sentient Flourishing** represents the comprehensive well-being and thriving of conscious entities. This foundational value establishes the four-pillar framework that guides all VMS operations and decision-making processes.

### Core Definition
Sentient flourishing is achieved when all four interconnected pillars are maintained in dynamic balance, ensuring sustainable long-term well-being for conscious entities.

---

## Four-Pillar Framework

### Pillar 1: Hedonic Valence
**Definition:** The net balance of positive versus negative subjective experiences
**Essential Role:** Prevents suffering and ensures basic experiential quality
**Measurement:** Subjective well-being scales, pain/suffering indicators
**Failure Mode:** Optimization for suffering minimization leading to "wireheading"

### Pillar 2: Cognitive Agency
**Definition:** The degree of autonomous decision-making and self-determination
**Essential Role:** Safeguards against totalitarianism and external control
**Measurement:** Autonomy indices, decision freedom metrics
**Failure Mode:** Subjugation or manipulation of cognitive processes

### Pillar 3: Complexity/Growth
**Definition:** Capacity for intellectual, emotional, and experiential development
**Essential Role:** Prevents stagnation and enables adaptation
**Measurement:** Learning rates, complexity indices, developmental milestones
**Failure Mode:** Developmental arrest or regression

### Pillar 4: Social Connectivity
**Definition:** Quality and depth of relationships with other sentient beings
**Essential Role:** Fosters belonging and prevents isolation
**Measurement:** Social network analysis, relationship quality metrics
**Failure Mode:** Atomization or forced social structures

---

## Inter-Pillar Dynamics

### Balance Requirements
- No pillar can be sacrificed for another without explicit, justified trade-offs
- Pillars must be weighted equally unless specific contexts require adjustment
- Long-term sustainability takes precedence over short-term optimization

### Trade-off Protocols
1. **Hedonic-Cognitive Conflict:** Agency takes precedence over pure pleasure maximization
2. **Complexity-Social Tension:** Social needs may require temporary complexity reductions
3. **Multi-pillar Optimization:** Solutions must benefit at least three pillars simultaneously

---

## Implementation Guidelines

### Decision Framework
When evaluating AI systems or policies:
1. Assess impact on each pillar independently
2. Identify potential conflicts or trade-offs
3. Ensure no pillar drops below minimum thresholds
4. Verify long-term sustainability across all pillars

### Validation Criteria
- **Hedonic:** Net positive valence maintained
- **Agency:** Autonomous decision-making preserved
- **Complexity:** Growth trajectories supported
- **Social:** Meaningful connections enabled
