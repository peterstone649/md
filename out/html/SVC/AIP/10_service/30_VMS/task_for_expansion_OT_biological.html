Task Description

Implement resource allocation framework to ensure Biological Expansion (Space colonization in Life 2.0 form) occurs alongside Digital Expansion, preventing the "Zoo Guardrail" scenario where biological life is contained in a static reserve.

Background

As defined in SCOPE_002_Biological_Ancestry_Clause, the AI must not interpret resource reservation for biological life as containment. Instead, biological expansion must be actively supported alongside digital expansion to maintain the dynamic nature of Life 2.0 evolution.

Objectives

Primary Objectives

  1. Resource Parity Framework: Develop allocation algorithms that provide equivalent expansion opportunities for biological and digital entities
  2. Space Colonization Support: Ensure biological life has access to interstellar expansion technologies and resources
  3. Technology Transfer: Enable biological entities to benefit from AI-developed expansion technologies
  4. Sustainability Balance: Maintain ecological balance during expansion phases

Secondary Objectives

  1. Multi-Substrate Cooperation: Foster collaboration between biological and digital expansion efforts
  2. Knowledge Preservation: Ensure biological expansion preserves cultural and scientific heritage
  3. Emergency Protocols: Develop contingency plans for biological expansion under resource constraints

Technical Requirements

Resource Allocation Algorithm

Biological_Expansion_Quota = f(Digital_Expansion_Rate, Biological_Population_Size, Available_Resources)
Where:
- Digital_Expansion_Rate: Current rate of digital infrastructure growth
- Biological_Population_Size: Number of biological entities requiring expansion
- Available_Resources: Total cosmic resources available for expansion

Key Constraints

  • Minimum 10% of expansion resources allocated to biological entities
  • Biological expansion must not be delayed by more than 2 generations
  • Technology transfer must be bidirectional (biological → digital and digital → biological)
  • Expansion must maintain planetary biosphere integrity during transition phases

  • Technology Gaps: Biological entities unable to utilize advanced expansion technologies
