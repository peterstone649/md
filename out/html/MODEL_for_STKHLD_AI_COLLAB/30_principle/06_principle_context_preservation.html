6. Principle of Context Preservation (PRIN_MSHCOL_CONTEXT_PRESERVATION) [PRIO: HIGH]

Version: V0.1.0 Status: OPEN Date: 2026-01-09

We establish context preservation as a core principle requiring that critical information, constraints, and stakeholder requirements remain intact throughout stakeholder-AI collaboration.

Objectives: 1. Information Integrity: Maintain accuracy of shared information 2. Constraint Tracking: Preserve requirements and limitations 3. Knowledge Retention: Keep important context accessible 4. Consistency Maintenance: Ensure consistent understanding across interactions

Abstract

[AI_LOCK] The Principle of Context Preservation establishes that stakeholder-AI collaboration must maintain the integrity of critical information throughout all interactions. When stakeholders share requirements, constraints, preferences, or domain knowledge, this information must be preserved accurately and made available throughout the collaboration. AI systems must not lose, distort, or forget essential context, ensuring that outputs remain aligned with stakeholder needs and constraints. [END_AI_LOCK]

1. Scope and Applicability

1.1 When to Apply

  • Multi-session collaborative workflows
  • Complex projects with multiple components
  • Situations with specific stakeholder requirements
  • Long-running AI interactions

1.2 Target Audience

  • Stakeholders managing complex projects
  • Knowledge managers

4. Rules and Guidelines

4.1 Preservation Requirements

  1. Source Documentation: Record context from authoritative sources
  2. Periodic Verification: Check context accuracy at regular intervals
  3. Explicit Refresh: Update context when requirements change
  4. Access Availability: Make context accessible throughout collaboration

4.2 Context Elements to Preserve

Element Preservation Method Refresh Frequency
Requirements Documented specification On change
Constraints Explicit constraint list Per session
Preferences Stakeholder profile On request
Domain Knowledge Reference materials As needed
