11. Convention for Confidence Scoring [CONV_FOR_MODELFW_CONFIDENCE_SCORING] [PRIO: HIGH]

Version: V0.1.0 Date: 2026-01-08

Purpose: Establish standardized confidence scoring criteria for framework components to ensure credible, differentiated, and evidence-based confidence assessments.

Confidence Score Scale

Score Level Evidence Required
0.95+ Very High Strong empirical validation, multiple independent confirmations, peer-reviewed consensus
0.85-0.94 High Theoretical foundation + some empirical support, limited independent validation
0.70-0.84 Moderate Theoretical foundation only, limited empirical validation
0.50-0.69 Low Preliminary, significant uncertainty, emerging theoretical support
<0.50 Very Low Speculative, limited theoretical basis, no empirical validation

Confidence Assessment Criteria

For Axioms:

  • 0.93-0.95: Strong philosophical foundation, multiple supporting frameworks, extensive logical analysis
  • 0.85-0.92: Solid theoretical basis, some empirical correlation, limited validation studies
  • 0.70-0.84: Conceptual framework, theoretical rationale, minimal validation
  • 0.50-0.69: Emerging hypothesis, limited supporting evidence

For Terms:

  • 0.90-0.95: Well-established in literature, widely accepted definition
  • 0.80-0.89: Standard definition, multiple authoritative sources
  • 0.65-0.79: Emerging usage, limited consensus
  • 0.50-0.64: Novel or contested concept

For Strategies:

  • 0.85-0.93: Proven methodology, multiple successful implementations
  • 0.75-0.84: Established approach, some case studies
  • 0.60-0.74: Theoretical framework, limited practice
  • 0.45-0.59: Novel approach, experimental

Confidence Assessment Format

**Component Confidence:** [SCORE] ([LEVEL])
- **Rationale:** [2-3 sentences explaining evidence basis]
- **Validation:** [Description of validation methods]
- **Contextual Stability:** [How stable is this assessment]
- **Practical Application:** [Relevance for practitioners]

Differentiation Guidelines

Do NOT assign the same confidence score to all components. - Base scores on actual evidence available - Higher scores require stronger justification - Acknowledge uncertainty where evidence is limited - Differentiate between theoretical and empirical support
