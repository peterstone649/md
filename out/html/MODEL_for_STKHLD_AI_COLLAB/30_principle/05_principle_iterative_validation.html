5. Principle of Iterative Validation (PRIN_MSHCOL_ITERATIVE_VALIDATION) [PRIO: HIGH]

Version: V0.1.0 Status: OPEN Date: 2026-01-09

We establish iterative validation as a core principle requiring systematic human review at critical checkpoints throughout stakeholder-AI collaboration.

Objectives: 1. Checkpoint Integration: Build validation points into collaborative workflows 2. Progressive Assurance: Increase validation rigor at key transitions 3. Error Detection: Identify and correct issues before they propagate 4. Quality Preservation: Maintain output quality through structured review

Abstract

[AI_LOCK] The Principle of Iterative Validation establishes that stakeholder-AI collaboration must include systematic human review at defined checkpoints throughout the workflow. Rather than relying solely on final review, this principle builds validation into the process itself, catching errors early and ensuring that AI-assisted outputs meet stakeholder requirements at each stage. Iterative validation combines human judgment with AI capability to produce higher quality outcomes. [END_AI_LOCK]
