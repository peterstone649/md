System [DEF_FOR_MFW_SYSTEM] [PRIO: HIGH]

  • Term: A system is an organized collection of interrelated components that work together to achieve a common purpose or function.
  • Description: Systems are fundamental organizational units that transform inputs into outputs through coordinated interactions between components. They maintain boundaries, exhibit emergent properties, and can be analyzed at multiple levels of complexity.

System Characteristics

Structural Elements

  • Components: Individual parts that make up the system
  • Relationships: Connections and interactions between components
  • Boundaries: Clear delineation of what is inside vs. outside the system
  • Hierarchy: Nested levels of organization and complexity

Functional Properties

  • Inputs: Resources or information entering the system
  • Processes: Transformations that occur within the system
  • Outputs: Results or products generated by the system
  • Feedback: Information loops that regulate system behavior

Emergent Properties

  • Holism: The whole is greater than the sum of its parts
  • Self-Organization: Systems can reorganize themselves in response to changes
  • Adaptation: Systems can modify their behavior based on experience
  • Resilience: Ability to maintain function despite disturbances

System Types

Open vs. Closed Systems

  • Open Systems: Exchange matter, energy, and information with their environment
  • Closed Systems: Exchange only energy with their environment
  • Isolated Systems: No exchange with their environment

Simple vs. Complex Systems

  • Simple Systems: Linear relationships, predictable behavior
  • Complex Systems: Non-linear relationships, emergent behavior, adaptive capacity
